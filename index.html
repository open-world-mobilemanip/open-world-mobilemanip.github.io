Learning Open World Mobile Manipulation of
Articulated Objects

CoRL 2023 Submission ID: 16

Abstract

We study the problem of mobile manipulation of articulated objects in the open world ranging from doors, and drawers to cabinets. We take a full-stack approach to tackle this problem. First, we propose a design of a mobile manipulator platform that can be easily assembled with an off-the-shelf 6-DOF robotic arm and a wheeled base with a total cost of under 20,000 USD. Secondly, we collect a large dataset of diverse real-world doors, drawers, and cabinets with a total of 37 real articulated objects across 7 different buildings using a mix of human teleoperation and scripted policies with a large number of successful examples. Finally, we propose a simple yet effective algorithmic framework based on transformers that achieve surprising generalization to new objects and new tasks. We plan to open-source all the components to allow the community to build upon these results toward making progress on this challenging problem.
